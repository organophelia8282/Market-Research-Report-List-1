<p><ul><li>Magnetic Electric Type</li><li>Hall Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The engine speed sensor market features two primary types: magnetic electric and Hall effect sensors. Magnetic electric sensors operate based on magnetic induction, generating a voltage signal proportional to rotational speed. They are commonly used for their reliability and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, Hall effect sensors utilize magnetic fields to produce output signals, offering high precision and sensitivity. Both types play crucial roles in monitoring engine performance, contributing to improved fuel efficiency, emissions control, and overall vehicle functionality.</p></p>
