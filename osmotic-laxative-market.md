<p><ul><li>Lactulose</li><li>Sorbitol</li><li>Magnesium Citrate</li><li>Milk of Magnesia</li><li>Polyethylene Glycol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The osmotic laxative market comprises several types, including Lactulose, Sorbitol, Magnesium Citrate, Milk of Magnesia, and Polyethylene Glycol. Lactulose is a synthetic sugar that draws water into the intestines, while Sorbitol is a sugar alcohol that also enhances intestinal moisture. Magnesium Citrate acts by pulling water into the bowel, promoting evacuation. Milk of Magnesia, primarily magnesium hydroxide, works similarly, and Polyethylene Glycol is a polyethylene glycol solution that maintains hydration in the intestines, facilitating regular bowel movements. Each type offers unique benefits for relieving constipation.</p></p>
