<p><p>Dental implants and prosthetics encompass various types designed to replace missing teeth. Endosteal implants are the most common, surgically placed into the jawbone, providing a stable foundation for crowns or dentures. Subperiosteal implants, on the other hand, are positioned under the gum but above the jawbone, making them suitable for patients with insufficient bone height. Both types aim to restore functionality and aesthetics, enhancing patient quality of life through reliable tooth replacement solutions.</p></p>
