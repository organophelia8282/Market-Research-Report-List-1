<p><p>Gram staining is a vital laboratory technique used to differentiate bacterial species into two categories: Gram-positive and Gram-negative, based on the characteristics of their cell walls. This method involves applying a crystal violet stain, followed by iodine, alcohol decolorization, and a counterstain (usually safranin). It plays a crucial role in microbiology, aiding in the identification and classification of bacteria, which is essential for diagnosing infections and planning treatment.</p><p>The Gram Staining Market is poised for significant growth, projected to expand at a CAGR of 8.6% during the forecast period. Emerging trends include the integration of digital technologies and automated systems in microbiological studies, which are streamlining workflows and improving diagnostic capabilities.
