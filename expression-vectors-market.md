<p><p>Expression vectors are tools used to introduce foreign DNA into host cells for protein production. The bacterial market utilizes vectors like plasmids for rapid and cost-effective protein synthesis. Mammalian expression systems are preferred for post-translational modifications and complex proteins, usually using vectors derived from viruses. Yeast vectors bridge the gap, offering both speed and eukaryotic-like modifications. Insect cell systems provide high yields and correct folding, making them suitable for complex eukaryotic proteins, especially in recombinant therapies.</p></p>
